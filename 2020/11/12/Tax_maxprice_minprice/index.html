w16_qp_23

  • Calculate the price elasticity of demand for meat in Bishkek

    • the price of meat increased by \(9\%\)

    • usually sell \(400\) kilos, now only \(250\) kilos

    • \[PED = \frac{\Delta \% QD}{\Delta \% P} = \frac{\frac{250-400}{400}}{9 \%} = (-)4.16\]
  • How does the price elasticity of demand for meat differ from the price elasticity of demand for flour in Bishkek?
    • \(PED_{meat}>1 \implies\) elastic demand

    • another shopkeeper increased what he charged for flour by \(15\%\), but sales did not decline by very much

      • the data indicates there is an inelastic demand for flour
  • How might an economist explain this difference
    • Numbers of substitutes
      • meat has many substitutes \(\implies\) PED tends to be more elastic since number of substitutes is a key determinant of PED
      • flour has fewer substitutes \(\implies\) PED tends to be less elastic / more inelastic
    • The portion of consumer spending
      • meat takes larger portion of consumer spending \(\implies\) more elastic
  • With the help of a supply and demand diagram, explain how the Russian central bank is "attempting to protect the value of the rouble"
    • 超出范围

w14_qp_23

  • What is the overall trend in UK real expenditure on alcohol purchased in shops for consumption off the premises between 2008 quarter 1 and 2012 quarter 2? [1]
    • downward
  • Explain how a change in one demand factor might have caused the change in weekly alcohol consumption for the 'average for all age groups' shown in Table 1 [2]
    • A fall in income
      • this means that consumers can afford less alcohol so this will reduce expenditure
    • A change in taste
      • there might be a change in consumers' taste, for instance, consumers may like to drink soft drinks more
    • A fall in the price of substitutes
      • consumers then tend to buy more substitutes, rather than alcohol
  • Identify the age group that has shown the greatest change in average weekly alcohol consumption between 2006 and 2010 [1]
    • \(16-24\) age group
  • For the age group you have identified, explain one possible economic reason for the change in its average weekly alcohol consumption compared with the other age groups [2]
    • a rise in unemployment amongest this age group
      • rise in unemployment \(\implies\) consumers can afford less alcohol
    • education campaigns aimed at younger age groups
      • thus, fewer consumers will be willing to buy alcohol with acknowledgement of health problems brought by alcohol
